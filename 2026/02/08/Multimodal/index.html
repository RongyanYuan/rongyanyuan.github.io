04. Multimodal and Cross-Modal Fusion

Multimodality means enabling a model to process multiple data types (text, images, audio, video) at once. By learning semantic relationships across modalities and mapping them into a shared representation space, the model can generate or understand cross-modal content. The outputs may be diverse, but they are not random—rather, they are reasonable generations grounded in the input information.

1. What is Multimodal

Multimodal technology refers to processing multiple types of data, such as text, audio, images, and video. A single-modality model handles only one data type, while multimodal systems aim to integrate them so a model can understand and generate across modalities.

In short, multimodality lets AI not only “see” or “hear,” but truly understand and operate across media, moving closer to human-like general intelligence.

2. Cross-Modal Mechanisms

Cross-modal learning focuses on how to fuse different data types into a shared representation. A typical pipeline includes feature extraction, vectorization, and multi-stage fusion.

2.1 Feature Extraction

Different modalities require different feature extractors:

Modality Common Methods
Text Transformer encoders (capture semantic relations)
Image / Video CNNs / Vision Transformers (visual features)
Audio STFT / Mel-spectrograms (frequency-domain features)

These features are then mapped into a shared embedding space, allowing cross-modal comparison and fusion.

2.2 High-Dimensional Vectorization and Similarity

After extraction, each modality is represented as a high-dimensional vector. In this space, the model can capture cross-modal similarity, such as matching images with descriptions or aligning audio with text.

2.3 Multi-Stage Fusion

Fusion often happens in stages rather than once:

  1. Early fusion: combine modalities at the input stage.
  2. Mid fusion: process independently, then interact in intermediate layers.
  3. Late fusion: generate separate outputs and combine at the end.

The best strategy depends on the task and compute constraints.

3. Multimodal Applications

  • Image → Text: image captioning and visual question answering
  • Text → Image / Audio: generative systems for art or speech
  • Cross-modal generation: text + image to video, or audio-conditioned visuals

Challenges remain, including high generation cost, long inference time, and consistency issues in long sequences (especially for video).

4. Summary

Multimodal technology is a foundation for AIGC. It enables AI to understand and generate across media, align different modalities semantically, and support complex applications like cross-modal search and content creation.

Key technical points:

  1. Feature extraction and shared mapping
  2. High-dimensional vectorization and similarity
  3. Multi-stage fusion strategies
  4. Optimizing cost and consistency
